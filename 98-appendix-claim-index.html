Appendix A — Appendix — Claim index (CL-IDs)

The 37 catalogued claims the review is built on · the link target for the inline claim-type tags

Every load-bearing sentence in this book carries an inline claim-type tag — e.g. (CL-P1-05, CONDITIONAL; G-04, G-14). The CL-Pn-NN part links here. This index lists the 37 catalogued claims (CL-ID), each with its claim-type (THEOREM / CONDITIONAL / EMPIRICAL / HEURISTIC / GAP-ASSERTION / POSITIONING), the chapter that hosts it, the guardrails that bind it (G-NN, defined in the guardrail callouts at the start of each chapter), and its statement. The chapter prose is the live realization of each row.

NoteClaim-type legend
  • THEOREM — an exact result established in companion manuscripts, cited motivationally only, never re-proved in this book.
  • GAP-ASSERTION — a negative existence claim supported by documented systematic absence (an exhaustive search), never a single citation. The two load-bearing ones are CL-P2-02 and CL-P4-06.
  • CONDITIONAL — holds under stated conditions, bound to those conditions in the same sentence.
  • EMPIRICAL — supported by named data/simulation evidence.
  • HEURISTIC — a reasoned argument / motivation, not a proof.
  • POSITIONING — a framing / contribution claim, each component attributed to its owner.

A.1 P0 — Foundational / inferential mismatch

Claim-ID Statement
CL-P0-01 The inferential mismatch the review addresses requires bridging three distinct literatures — robust cluster-sandwich estimation (P1), Bayesian hierarchical/multilevel meta-analysis (P2), and the calibrated-Bayes philosophy of preserving the inference center while realigning covariance (P3) — each necessary but individually insufficient to define a Bayesian RVE. (Type - HEURISTIC; Chapter - Ch1; Guardrails - G-24)
CL-P0-02 The frequentist RVE lineage made the robust sandwich the structural standard for dependent-effect meta-analysis (Hedges et al., 2010; Pustejovsky & Chen, 2024), and calibrated-Bayes theory (Little, 2012; Rubin, 1984) supplies the intellectual license to import the RVE/CHE sandwich covariance target into Bayesian inference — yet we found no published Bayesian meta-analysis method that joins those streams in the G-27 sense. (Type - POSITIONING; Chapter - Ch1; Guardrails - G-23, G-24, G-27)

A.2 P1 — The frequentist RVE lineage owns it

Claim-ID Statement
CL-P1-01 Robust variance estimation (RVE) is the established frequentist field-default for meta-analysis with dependent effect sizes, built from the cluster-sandwich and refined across the lineage Hedges et al. (2010) → Tipton (2015) → Tipton & Pustejovsky (2015) → (Pustejovsky & Tipton, 2018, 2022) → Pustejovsky & Chen (2024). (Type - POSITIONING; Chapter - Ch2; Guardrails - —)
CL-P1-02 The RVE sandwich targets the marginal covariance of the effect-size vector via a working model (aggregated or correlated-effects) that may misspecify the within-study correlation, and is asymptotically consistent in m (the number of studies) without requiring the true within-study correlation to be known. (Type - CONDITIONAL; Chapter - Ch2; Guardrails - G-03, G-15)
CL-P1-03 The sandwich is consistent under arbitrary misspecification of the within-study correlation structure (which is almost never known), provided the working model’s marginal moments are calibrated to the data-generating process — the misspecification-robustness that motivates covariance recalibration. (Type - CONDITIONAL; Chapter - Ch2; Guardrails - G-25)
CL-P1-04 Small-m RVE inflates Type I error (especially m < 8) unless a named small-sample correction is applied — HTZ, AHZ, CWB, or CR2 (Bell–McCaffrey); RVE without a named correction does not control Type I error in small-m meta-analysis. (Type - EMPIRICAL; Chapter - Ch2; Guardrails - G-15)
CL-P1-05 CR2 (Bell–McCaffrey / Imbens & Kolesár (2016)) improves coverage on balanced designs but degrades under severe imbalance or skewed regressors, and its compound-symmetric / constant-subgroup-size conditions are not always met; CR2 is a design-specific tuning and an external comparator, not a universal fix or the calibration target. (Type - CONDITIONAL; Chapter - Ch2; Guardrails - G-04, G-14)
CL-P1-06 Dependent effects are handled by either the aggregated (one-row-per-study) or correlated-effects (observation-level) working model; when moderators vary within study the aggregated representation ceases to exist, whereas the correlated-effects model remains well-defined with the sandwich applied at the observation level. (Type - CONDITIONAL; Chapter - Ch2; Guardrails - G-05)
CL-P1-07 The Satterthwaite degrees-of-freedom approximation is routinely applied in frequentist RVE meta-regression to set effective df for tests/CIs; it is asymptotically justified but biased under severe imbalance or small m. (Type - CONDITIONAL; Chapter - Ch2; Guardrails - G-15)
CL-P1-08 clubSandwich (Pustejovsky & Tipton) and robumeta (Fisher & Tipton) are the canonical software implementations of frequentist RVE with small-sample corrections (CR2, HTZ/AHZ, Satterthwaite df) and are the field-standard tooling for dependent-effect meta-analysis. (Type - POSITIONING; Chapter - Ch2; Guardrails - —)

A.3 P2 — Bayesian meta-analysis & the missing analogue

Claim-ID Statement
CL-P2-01 Bayesian meta-analysis is modeling-centric and genuinely flexible — weakly informative priors, hierarchical random-effects, multivariate/correlated-effects, three-level, network, and location-scale heterogeneity models — and that prior/structural flexibility is real and well-documented. (Type - EMPIRICAL; Chapter - Ch3; Guardrails - —)
CL-P2-02 We found no published Bayesian meta-analysis method that targets the RVE/CHE sandwich covariance as an explicit calibration target (in the frequentist-RVE sense), realigned under a Little/Rubin calibrated-Bayes license. (Type - GAP-ASSERTION; Chapter - Ch3 / Ch6; Guardrails - G-23, G-27)
CL-P2-03 Because the Bayesian analyst keeps the posterior as the primary inferential object, the frequentist sandwich — though theoretically motivated — does not by itself supply a named posterior target a Bayesian workflow can adopt; the calibration object (a posterior with a specific sandwich-calibrated covariance) is not a standard object in the Bayesian meta-analysis literature. (Type - POSITIONING; Chapter - Ch3; Guardrails - G-23)
CL-P2-04 Cheung’s SEM–inverse-variance equivalence requires the Level-1 error variance be fixed equal to one and does not hold under heteroscedasticity; SEM-based meta-analysis (Cheung, 2008, 2013) is therefore not a universal equivalent to frequentist RVE. (Type - CONDITIONAL; Chapter - Ch3; Guardrails - G-26)
CL-P2-05 Multivariate/multilevel Bayesian meta-analysis (Fernández-Castilla et al., 2021; Jackson et al., 2011; Van Den Noortgate et al., 2015) models within- and between-study dependence hierarchically and preserves observation-level design matrices when moderators vary within study, yet none pairs that structure with the calibrated-Bayes realignment of posterior covariance to a frequentist-RVE sandwich target. (Type - CONDITIONAL; Chapter - Ch3; Guardrails - G-23)
CL-P2-06 Recent (2023–2025) Bayesian network meta-analysis integrating composite-likelihood / calibrated-Bayesian ideas (e.g., Wang et al., 2025) is an active methodological frontier, but these entries do not target the RVE/CHE sandwich covariance as an explicit calibration target under a Little/Rubin calibrated-Bayes license. (Type - CONDITIONAL; Chapter - Ch3; Guardrails - G-23, G-27)

A.4 P3 — The calibrated-Bayes lens

Claim-ID Statement
CL-P3-01 The calibrated-Bayes principle — Bayesian models judged against frequentist operating characteristics, “preserve the center, realign the covariance” — is established prior art in Little (2012) and Rubin (1984). (Type - CONDITIONAL; Chapter - Ch4; Guardrails - G-24)
CL-P3-02 Under the calibrated-Bayes license, an affine recoloring preserves the posterior mean while realigning the marginal covariance toward a frequentist target. (Type - HEURISTIC; Chapter - Ch4; Guardrails - G-21, G-24)
CL-P3-03 Williams & Savitsky (2021) / Savitsky & Williams (2022) established the survey-estimation↔︎Bayesian-paradigm bridge via a Cholesky scale-and-shape projection that double-weights both the unit-level likelihood and the random-effect (group) prior; they never mention meta-analysis, and their bridge is approximate/asymptotic. (Type - CONDITIONAL; Chapter - Ch4; Guardrails - G-01, G-02, G-07, G-08)
CL-P3-04 The Godambe sandwich / estimating-equation substrate (Binder, 1983; Varin, 2008; Varin et al., 2011) supplies the mathematical justification for treating covariance realignment as principled rather than ad hoc under model misspecification. (Type - HEURISTIC; Chapter - Ch4; Guardrails - G-25)
CL-P3-05 Composite likelihood (Varin, 2008; Varin et al., 2011) is asymptotic and assumes estimable low-dimensional margins; under unknown within-study correlations its operating guarantees degrade — motivating, but not supplying, a covariance correction for dependent meta-analytic effects. (Type - CONDITIONAL; Chapter - Ch4; Guardrails - G-25)
CL-P3-06 The unit-weight boundary (w_ij = 1 and w_θ = 1) is the configuration at which a posterior affinely recolored by the marginal RVE sandwich achieves an exact finite-sample identity between the posterior’s marginal covariance and the RVE target — this review’s own result, not S&W’s asymptotic mechanism. (Type - THEOREM; Chapter - Ch4; Guardrails - G-08, G-16, G-17)
CL-P3-07 CCC (Cholesky Calibration Correction) is this review’s own coinage for the meta-analytic operationalization of the calibrated-Bayes principle, implementing the Williams & Savitsky scale-and-shape (Cholesky) projection mechanism. (Type - POSITIONING; Chapter - Ch4; Guardrails - G-06)
CL-P3-08 CCC calibration is blockwise by design (β-block Cholesky on the marginal sandwich target; θ-block diagonal CE rescaling); the full-joint (β, θ) sandwich is structurally rank-deficient (rank ≤ J < K + J, incidental-parameter geometry), so full-joint Cholesky calibration is not attempted — a principled design choice, not a failure. (Type - CONDITIONAL; Chapter - Ch4 / Ch5; Guardrails - G-10, G-11)
CL-P3-09 We found no prior Bayesian meta-analysis paper that has applied the calibrated-Bayes principle (“preserve center, realign covariance”) to the RVE/CHE sandwich covariance as an explicit calibration target; this bridge from calibrated-Bayes philosophy to dependent-effect meta-analysis is this review’s contribution. (Type - POSITIONING; Chapter - Ch4 / Ch6; Guardrails - G-01, G-23, G-24, G-27)
CL-P3-10 In the CCC working model, ρ is the conditional residual (Level-1) working correlation of y_j given (β, θ_j) — not the marginal intra-class correlation, which is inflated by τ² through Σ_j(τ²) = V_j + τ²·11ᵀ; the fixed-ρ / equicorrelation specification is a working-model / reporting-scope choice, not the logical site where exactness arises. (Type - CONDITIONAL; Chapter - Ch4 / Ch5; Guardrails - G-19, G-20)
CL-P3-11 CCC (Cholesky Calibration Correction) fills the identified gap: it matches a named CR2 / RVE sandwich covariance exactly (finite-sample, at the unit-weight boundary) while leaving coverage as a separately demonstrated, asymptotic operating characteristic — the affirmative contribution statement that closes the thesis spine. (Type - POSITIONING; Chapter - Ch6; Guardrails - G-06, G-09, G-16)

A.5 P4 — Why Bayesian RVE specifically

Claim-ID Statement
CL-P4-01 A specifically Bayesian RVE delivers three things frequentist RVE cannot easily provide: flexible prior modeling of heterogeneity, preservation of observation-level multilevel structure, and predictor-specific (per-coefficient) diagnostics. (Type - POSITIONING; Chapter - Ch5; Guardrails - —)
CL-P4-02 Flexible Bayesian prior modeling of heterogeneity is the natural launchpad for Bayesian network-meta-analysis extensions. (Type - CONDITIONAL; Chapter - Ch5; Guardrails - —)
CL-P4-03 The unaggregated (observation-level) Bayesian likelihood preserves within-study design matrices even when moderators vary within study — the regime where the frequentist aggregated representation ceases to exist. (Type - CONDITIONAL; Chapter - Ch5; Guardrails - G-05)
CL-P4-04 A per-coefficient design-effect-ratio (DER) diagnostic reveals which coefficients need correction, enabling selective predictor-specific variance correction — a capability frequentist RVE (all-or-nothing) does not easily provide. (Type - POSITIONING; Chapter - Ch5; Guardrails - G-22)
CL-P4-05 The MCMC-vs-CCC design-effect-ratio comparison is a data-driven diagnostic for identifying correction-sensitive parameters in meta-analytic dependent-effect inference; this review motivates it and points to a forthcoming companion paper, which develops it. (Type - POSITIONING; Chapter - Ch5; Guardrails - G-22)
CL-P4-06 We found no published method that combines MCMC-based Bayesian inference with per-coefficient design-effect-ratio diagnostics for meta-analytic dependent effect sizes. (Type - GAP-ASSERTION; Chapter - Ch5 / Ch6; Guardrails - G-22, G-23)
CL-P4-07 Theorem 1 (Conditional Identity):** given a correctly specified within-study covariance and unit weights, the Cholesky-affine calibrated posterior’s covariance matches the target RVE sandwich covariance exactly in finite samples, over the matched equicorrelation family V_j = a_j C_j(ρ) (compound-symmetric class). (Type - THEOREM; Chapter - Ch5; Guardrails - G-16, G-17)
CL-P4-08 Theorem 2 (Marginal Identity):** the calibrated posterior’s marginal covariance (integrating out the random intercepts θ_j) matches the known marginal RVE sandwich target exactly under unit weights, for any matched marginal Gaussian kernel — strictly more general than Theorem 1. (Type - THEOREM; Chapter - Ch5; Guardrails - G-18)
CL-P4-09 Corollary 1 (Identity Under Correct Specification):** under correct model specification with unit weights, the calibrated and uncalibrated posterior covariances coincide — the correction is neutral when the working model is right. (Type - THEOREM; Chapter - Ch5; Guardrails - G-12)
CL-P4-10 Covariance-matching (the three CCC theorems) is an exact finite-sample algebraic property; coverage is a separate operating-characteristic claim, asymptotic in J and demonstrated only by simulation. (Type - EMPIRICAL; Chapter - Ch5; Guardrails - G-09, G-13)
