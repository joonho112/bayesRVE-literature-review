  • G-16 (the unit-weight boundary is the exact contribution, not a limitation). The exact finite-sample matching identity holds at the unit-weight boundary over the matched equicorrelation family. That boundary is the site of an exact theoretical result, this review’s contribution, never a restriction adopted for tractability.   • G-17 (equicorrelation-family scope, not arbitrary SPD). The exact conditional identity is proven over the matched equicorrelation family \(V_j = a_j C_j(\rho)\) with arbitrary per-cluster scale \(a_j > 0\), not over arbitrary heteroscedastic or symmetric-positive-definite (SPD) within-study covariance; the SPD generalization is named as an open extension, never as a covered case.
  • G-18 (the marginal identity is strictly more general). Covariance-matching is an exact, finite-sample, algebraic identity against the named CR2/RVE sandwich target; coverage is a separate operating characteristic: asymptotic in \(J\), simulation-shown. The two live in separate sentences, and the matching identity is never read as a coverage guarantee.

The simulation evidence (TSL15 / sim3, intercept-only) is proof-of-concept under specified data-generating processes, where the calibrated intervals are near nominal; multivariate coefficient-level behavior is named as open, and no bare coverage percentage appears.

A reviewer who has followed the funnel — the frequentist robust-variance-estimation (RVE) lineage owns the marginal RVE/CHE sandwich covariance as the target (Chapter 2), the Bayesian literature left it unoccupied as a named posterior calibration object (Chapter 3), the calibrated-Bayes principle of Little and Rubin licensed its import (Chapter 4), and Cholesky Calibration Correction (CCC) delivers the join (Chapter 5) — is owed an exact statement of where each result holds. This chapter supplies it. Each boundary doubles as an open question this review has chosen not to pre-resolve, and the funnel then closes affirmatively.

7.1 7.1 Scope of the exact identity

Scope echo of CL-P3-06 and CL-P4-07/08 — no new CL-ID; the scope clauses are hosted at full budget in Chapters 4 and 5.

The exact finite-sample identity holds at the unit-weight boundary over the matched equicorrelation family \(V_j = a_j C_j(\rho)\). This is the configuration, with both the unit-level likelihood weight and the random-effect (group) prior weight set to one, at which a posterior affinely recolored by the marginal RVE sandwich attains an exact, finite-sample equality between the calibrated posterior’s covariance and the named CR2/RVE sandwich target. That boundary is an exact theoretical result and an original contribution, not a restriction imposed for tractability (established in a companion manuscript, Theorems 1–2 and Corollary 1, for the unit-weight bridge, the marginal fixed-effect sandwich target, and the center-preserving recoloring; and a companion technical report, Theorem 2, for the exact covariance-matching mechanics, cited motivationally). The exactness is distinct in kind from the asymptotic Williams and Savitsky survey mechanism that motivates it. That mechanism is large-sample; the unit-weight identity is an original finite-sample result.

The scope of that exactness is the matched equicorrelation family, with arbitrary per-cluster scale \(a_j > 0\), not arbitrary SPD within-study covariance. The conditional bridge (companion manuscript, Theorem 1) is proven over the compound-symmetric class \(V_j = a_j C_j(\rho)\); nonuniform weights and arbitrary heteroscedastic or SPD within-study covariance are scoped-out future extensions, named openly, not hidden constraints (G-17). Naming this scope precisely keeps the result from inviting the “real sampling variances are heteroscedastic” objection: the equicorrelation family is the reporting scope at which the stated theorem holds, and the fixed-\(\rho\) specification is a working-model choice, not the logical site where exactness arises.

The marginal result is strictly more general than the conditional one, and the two scopes are not flattened (G-18). The companion manuscript’s marginal fixed-effect sandwich identity (Theorem 2) establishes that, under unit weights and the matched translation, the meta-analytic and survey interpretations share the same marginal fixed-effect sandwich target after the random intercepts \(\theta_j\) are integrated out. Because it rests only on equality of matched marginal Gaussian densities with no compound-symmetric requirement, the marginal identity dominates the conditional/equicorrelation bridge in generality rather than sharing its scope. The asymmetry is the precise statement: the equicorrelation scope governs the conditional bridge, while the marginal identity reaches any matched marginal Gaussian kernel. Read together, these are not two limitations but two exact results at two clearly marked scopes: the coordinates of the contribution, stated as preconditions.

7.2 7.2 Conditional on \(\omega^2\); covariance-matching is not coverage; the multivariate question open

Scope echo of CL-P4-09/10 — no new CL-ID; the scope clauses are hosted at full budget in Chapter 5.

The matching results are conditional on the plug-in \(\omega^2\) and the within-study correlation. The identity-under-correct-specification result makes the conditionality concrete: when the MCMC posterior covariance already equals the target covariance, the correction is the identity transformation, neutral when the working model is right (a companion technical report, Theorem 3). The reach of that neutrality is bounded by its condition. If \(\omega^2\) is badly misspecified, the target itself is wrong, and this review claims no data-driven self-correction for a misspecified \(\omega^2\) (G-12). This is the honest precondition of any plug-in calibration: the correction realigns the posterior toward a named target; it does not certify the target was correctly specified. Naming it keeps the contribution exact rather than overstated.

The most consequential separation in this book is between two different kinds of claim, and the boundary holds them apart in separate sentences (G-09). Covariance-matching, as established by the three CCC results, is an exact finite-sample algebraic property of the calibrated posterior’s covariance relative to the named CR2/RVE sandwich target. Coverage is a separate operating characteristic: it is asymptotic in \(J\) and demonstrated only by simulation under specified data-generating processes, where the calibrated intervals are near nominal. The two claims are distinct. Exact algebraic matching does not by itself imply nominal interval coverage, and the one is never read into the other. The simulation evidence is proof-of-concept under the conditions studied, intercept-only, not universal (G-13); no specific coverage percentage is asserted, only the qualitative “near nominal.” The boundary frames a research agenda: multivariate coefficient-level operating characteristics (how the calibrated intervals behave for a full vector of meta-regression coefficients) are an open question this review does not pre-resolve. The exact matching is established at the stated scope today; the operating characteristics beyond the intercept-only regime are the next thing to demonstrate, named as open rather than quietly assumed.

7.3 7.3 The per-coefficient DER: motivated here, developed in a forthcoming companion paper

Scope echo of CL-P4-04/05 — no new CL-ID; the scope clauses are hosted at full budget in Chapter 5.

The final boundary is a hand-off, and it points forward to a developed contribution rather than to an absence. The per-coefficient design-effect-ratio (DER) diagnostic — which identifies which coefficients require variance correction and thereby enables selective, predictor-specific correction rather than the all-or-nothing application the frequentist sandwich provides — is established only at survey scope by Lee (2026, p. arXiv:2603.07791), who contrasts the MCMC posterior variance against a design-corrected sandwich target and performs no meta-analysis. The contrast is a design-class difference, framed neutrally, not a deficiency of the frequentist standard, and it is motivated here, not delivered here (G-22). The diagnostic is not absent from the present program: current bayesRVE outputs already include scoped DER-adjacent quantities (DER_mu, the \(\theta\) summaries, the CCC scale factors r_k, and the CHE-diagonal DER_emp). These existing outputs are narrower than the full workflow, not a placeholder for it: the complete contrast-level/default reporting workflow for meta-analytic dependent effects (the MCMC-versus-CCC comparison for pre-specified contrasts with named variance targets that would operationalize selective correction across a coefficient vector) is the scope of a forthcoming companion paper. This review motivates the diagnostic from its Bayesian survey-scope origin and points to that work; it does not pre-empt it.

With these three coordinates fixed, the funnel closes affirmatively. The boundaries are not concessions subtracted from the contribution; they are its exact coordinates. The frequentist RVE lineage fixed the marginal RVE/CHE sandwich covariance as the target (Pillar 1); the Bayesian literature left it unoccupied as a named posterior calibration object (Pillar 2); the calibrated-Bayes principle of Little and Rubin supplied the license to import it (Pillar 3); and CCC delivers the join, implementing the Williams and Savitsky Cholesky mechanism under that license to match the named CR2/RVE sandwich covariance exactly in finite samples at the unit-weight boundary over the matched equicorrelation family (Pillar 4). Coverage remains throughout a separate operating characteristic, asymptotic in \(J\) and simulation-shown, never inferred from the matching identity; and the full contrast-level per-coefficient diagnostic workflow is motivated here and developed in a forthcoming companion paper. The contribution stands exactly where its boundaries place it.
